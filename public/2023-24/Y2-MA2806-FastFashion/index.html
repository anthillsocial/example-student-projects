Global Fast Fashion

Fast fashion is defined as high fashion at a low cost. Manufacturers mass-produce their clothing supplies, which are ever-changing in alignment with the latest seasonal trends; the overproduction of supplies results in retailers becoming overstocked, consequently leading to enormous volumes of textiles waste. As of August 2022, 92 million tonnes of garments end up in landfills (of the 100 billion garments that are produced globally each year). Some of the largest fast fashion giants include the Inditex Group brands (Zara, Bershka, Stradivarius, Pull & Bear, Massimo Dutti, and Oysho), H&M, Uniqlo, Shein, GAP, Primark, and ASOS.

FAST FASHION IN THE UK

Fast fashion is a significant part of the UK fashion industry. It's popular amongst consumers because it can offer fashion at a low price. Roughly 1.051 million tonnes of clothes are purchased in the UK each year. 360,000 tonnes of textiles that are thrown away each year in the UK is made up of clothes. The reason for this is because the population wants to respond to current fashion trends and as a result of this, a clothing garment in worn 10 times on average before it is discarded.

ENVIRONMENTAL IMPACTS OF FAST FASHION

When it comes to negative environmental impact, the fashion industry comes in the rankings at fourth. One of the major factors as to why our oceans are having plastics washed up in them is because of the synthetic textiles that these clothes are made out of. Nylon, polyester and acrylic are released into the oceans through the washing of items. The production of fast fashion also uses approximately 93 billion cubic metres of water every year and the World Bank has claimed that 20% of industrial water waste pollution worldwide derives from the fashion industry.

THE IMPORTANCE OF SUSTAINABILITY

Investing in ethical fashion not only contributes to the health of the planet but also the environment that those who produce the fashion work in. Fast fashion companies often may their employees work in poor safety conditions and pay them a wage that they're barely able to live on. The workers are also exploited as they're forced to work long hours and pressured into manufactoring mass amounts of garments everyday. By not buying into these companies, you're not supporting the mistreatment of workers and paying for them to be exploited.

We can also reduce the amount of waste that is generated through buying from sustainable brands. The global production of solid waste is expected to reach a new high of 3.4 billion metric tonnes by 2050. Sustainable brands aim to reduce waste by manufacturing clothes that use long-lasting textiles. This means that consumers are purchasing higher quality clothing that can be reused and should not have to be wasted for a long time.

Sustainable fashion aditionally uses biodegradable materials sourced from natural or recyled fibres. In order to grow, these materials don't require any fertiliser, they use less water and energy and don't use any chemical treatment. This results in the reduction of their carbon footprint.

SUSTAINABLE UK CLOTHING BRANDS

Here are some brands that produce high quality, sustainable clothing.

1. FANFARE

Fanfare uses circular collections which means that they aim to remove non eco-friendly materials from the supply chain, they return necessary waste to nature safely. They have a "deep respect for both people and the planet"

2. YES FRIENDS

Yes Friends is a brand who makes ethical clothing affordable. They pride themselves in producing at scale and being able to pay their workers a livable wage. Additionally, they use sustainable manufacturing methods and lower direct to consumer margins which enables them to sell at a lower price.

3. PANGAIA

Pangaia is a company who have combined roles of scientists, technologists and designers to help solve the environmental problem of the clothing industry. Their vision is to "inspire and accelerate an Earth positive future". They use science to carefully choose the materials that the clothes will be made out of to promote biodiversity on Earth.
