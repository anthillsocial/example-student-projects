Chapter 8: A wolf in sheeps clothing

For my last poem request I gave the machine the ultimate question to explore in its creation.

The result that came out was by far the best piece of work I had yet seen from a machine.

One portion that stood out to me immediately was “A poem of longing flows from cold circuits, // all of the words, but none of the wounds within.” The second line in particular left me frozen for a few seconds. While it does not particularly shine in terms of literary devices, it certainly feels quite shocking considering the context: a machine writing a cautionary poem about the pollution of AI, and within it, acknowledging its own inability to fully feel the extent of the fear. Words without wounds, without feelings, which is exactly what this poem is and the machine knows it.


Perhaps it was me reading into it too much - reflecting my own human emotions onto, what is most definitely, an empty string of text - or maybe my expectations had been lowered by the hundreds of subpar results I had gotten so far. After reading it a few times I certainly noticed some repetitions and cliches, which quickly knocked this piece off of its piedestal. But I couldn’t help but wonder if these hiccups only made the poem feel more real. After all, I am not a professional poet and can barely call myself amateur, so not having a “perfect” piece of work may have worked in the bot’s favour.
Based on this piece, taking everything I had learned about AI generated poetry so far, I created my own version of this poem, trying to mimic the piece as much as possible, while still keeping mine original.
"We step forth into an unknown plane.
Surrender all will to ease our pain.
Convinced that our problems can be solved with numbers,
We replace our limbs with wires and cogs.

One can never be certain of the voice inside,
For the knowledge we feast upon
Is dispatched to us by hollow vessels.
Their synthetic taste buds—unable to dispute lie from truth.

Unexpectedly, a new sun dawned on our nation.
It is them we strung up with our own hands—
Filled with stuffing, molded up—yet the word puppet
Only comes to mind when we look in the mirror.
Their posterior is made of plastic—yet the texture we’re born with
Is lasered away for the sake of aesthetics.
The decals we’ve scored from our own visage,
We self-consciously adhere back onto our skin.

Will the prickly buzz of voltage pulsing through the wires,
Muffle the heavy beat echoing through ventricles?
Can the artificial utterances, spliced up for convenience,
Warm up the skin like a blanket of spoken love?
Unsatisfied with scepticism from our brethren,
We replace fortune tellers with algorithms—
Praying that our fates could be changed with code.

Once the dusk finally curtains what remains of the sky—
No air will be spared that could fill our lungs.
The circuits will carry on their endless waltz.
And the dust will settle on a nation of code."
I utilised all basic giveaways of AI text, such as the extended dashes, the unnecessarily complex vocab, repetition of theme-specific words, and, of course, mentions of the gorgeous sunlight. This was somewhat easier to write compared to my previous attempts, as I didn’t have to follow a constraining rhyming scheme or rhythmic structure. The hardest part was looking up good synonyms in the dictionary to imitate the bot’s ability to sound smarter than it actually is. In the end, I felt like my poem ended up being much more critical of the theme at hand, as I touched upon issues the machine did not mention, nor do I think is aware of yet.
