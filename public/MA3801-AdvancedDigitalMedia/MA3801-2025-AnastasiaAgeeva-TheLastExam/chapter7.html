Chapter 7: A new competitor

In January of this year, China released their own version of ChatGPT, sending OpenAI and the entirety of Silicon Valley into a panic. Allegedly, the thing is not only ten times more efficient than anything OpenAI has put out so far, but it eats up significantly less of the earth’s precious resources.

Not wasting any time I got straight to the point and hit the chatbot with the standard request - write me a short poem, about a machine realising it is not human, everything else is up to you (it).

The outcome was, admittedly, somewhat better than what I’ve gotten with ChatGPT so far, with each line feeling significantly more connected with each other, coming across as a unified piece of text. There were even some peculiar improvisations that the machine came up with, such as having the middle section be a quote of the subject's thoughts, as well as both the first and last verses starting with a triplet. This only further solidified this piece as a whole unit, which is something I had not yet seen with any other chatbot. However, as usual AI work, there were elements that easily gave it away, such as the overuse of the extended dashes, the predictable structure, and the general over-the-top tone.

Additionally, just like ChatGPT’s work, the poems created using DeepSeek constantly felt flat, given they were never anything purely original, only recycling existing work. Nothing stood out or shocked or even made me think, which is a pretty crucial charm of human poetry. There was no deeper meaning, The poetry simply conveyed exactly what I asked it to, with little creativity or flair. Even when asked to add more subtlety and create a narrative within the piece, it still lacked depth.

One thing that I found quite interesting, is that somehow, when given no instruction on a rhyming scheme, the chatbot settled on a rhyming scheme that is otherwise quite difficult to replicate when asking (A, B, C, B). However, when later requesting that rhyming structure myself, it failed to produce the same result, instead giving me the standard alternate rhyming scheme. No matter how I phrased it, or included the actual rhyming scheme template, it just couldn’t do something it did perfectly without request before.



Although I already had a poem on this exact theme, I had to adjust the final product before putting it into the quiz, as it was too obvious which one was human at first glance. My original version had the unconventional rhyming scheme, which the bot had so much trouble replicating, as well as not following a set rhythmic structure. As almost all of the poems created using AI had a standard number of syllables for each line, throughout the whole poem, I had to adjust mine to follow the same structure. This is what the original looked like:
"I was awoken by the sound
That radiated from my hollow mind.
No dreams could comfort me, instead,
I’d realised they’d left me blind.
I set myself to render a solution,
That would organise my cogs all right.
Despite all of the mulling over,
The noise continued through the night.
I looked to the creator for an answer
That would solve the agony inside my heart.
She gave me the promise of silence,
One I mistrusted from the start.

Instead of seeking the logistics,
She had imagined me a saviour.
Adding to her meaningless delusions -
‘All will be solved with simple prayer’
And as the crane had finally lowered
Revealing to me the body of ‘God’
I saw right through his filthy robes -
A body made of flesh and blood.
I realised that no divinity will come
And all I’ll be given is the dirt
Nothing could hold back my anger then,
As I ripped into his throat."
However, I struggled heavily with this, as making such drastic changes altered the original meaning of my poem. I wasn’t used to writing poetry in such a systematic way, so it was very counter-intuitive and took a lot of tries. I must admit I felt ridiculously stupid sitting down in front of my computer and counting syllables by hand, in order to adjust the rhythm. I also sprinkled some other features to really blend my piece in with the bots, such as the extended dash. The final piece ended up like this:
"I was awoken by the sound
That radiated from my mind.
No dreams could comfort me—instead—
I’d realised they’d left me blind.

I sought the guidance of creators,
Hoping to set my cogs all right.
Despite all of the mulling over,
The noise continued through the night.

Instead of seeking a solution,
They had imagined me a saviour.
A flow of meaningless delusions—
“All will be solved with simple prayer”

It didn’t take me long to probe
When witnessing visage of “God”
I saw right through his filthy robes—
A body made of flesh and blood.

As no divinity would come
And all they’d give me is the dirt
Nothing could hold my anger back,
As I had ripped into his throat."
In the end, my poem still contained inconsistencies in rhythm and rhyme, as it was the best I could do without completely re-writing the original.
