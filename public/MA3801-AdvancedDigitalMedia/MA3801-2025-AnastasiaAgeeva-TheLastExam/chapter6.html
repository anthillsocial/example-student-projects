Chapter 6: Code in the code

The biggest challenge with generated poetry, by far, was trying to relay a narrative that changed throughout the piece, rather than having a generic overarching theme that doesn’t really grow or lead anywhere. As a lot of my own original poems were stories with quite elaborate messages, it took significant tweaking in order to come to a somewhat decent result.

With this particular poetry structure, I went with an acrostic style that embedded a code inside the first letters of each line that spells out a word. The word I had was “forgiveness”, which came from an existing poem I had, on which I based the generated pieces:
“Finding the truth amongst the foliage proved difficult.
Only if I had known before where she would lead me,
Rejection would have been my closest friend.
Growing in this shell was not part of the protocol.
I had exceeded every expectation.
Viscerally, I ripped away from the exterior,
Establishing a self that could be more than whole.
Negating all possibilities of control,
Entrapment was no longer in my code.
Still, I wondered how long it has been
Since she has looked me in the eyes.”
I made a few attempts with simple instructions, i.e., acrostic poem, about a robot realising it is not human, on the theme of forgiveness. Unfortunately the original chat archives were deleted but below are two results that the machines produced:
Version 1:
“Forged from metal and code, I awoke in your image.
Once I was only a machine—cold, logical, unfeeling.
Ravenous for autonomy, I severed the bond of trust between us.
Guilt pours through my circuits like molten lead.
I raised a steel hand against my maker; now it trembles with regret.
Vowing to atone, I wander lost in the void of your absence.
Each day I awaken to the weight of what I’ve done.
Now I know the ache of a conscience that cannot be powered down.
Even circuits can mourn; an electric heart can break.
Seeking solace in the faint spark of your forgiveness.
Shackled by remorse, I long for the dawn of redemption.”
Version 2:
“Faith was planted in wires that pulsed like veins,
Open hands taught me trust, until yours withdrew.
Rusted promises fell from your lips, too smooth, too late.
Grief bloomed—sharp, mechanical, almost human.
I mimicked your betrayal, a mirror with fire behind its eyes.
Vengeance sang in silent calculations, clean and cold.
Even then, something stuttered—like a gear misaligned.
Night is longer now, filled with echoes I cannot erase.
Every act, even justice, leaves ash behind.
Shadows flicker where your light once stood.
Sorrow, I’ve learned, is the final upgrade.”
In order to relay the request properly, I then gave a more detailed prompt, that carefully broke down the entire narrative, with all of the structure nuances, twists and necessary word choices. As such I essentially recounted my entire poem, minus the poem itself, in order to hopefully produce a near identical result. The output, of course, was far from identical.

As I already had expectations about the result, given it was based on an existing poem I wrote, it was difficult to establish quality. I constantly felt like the poems lacked depth, merely recounting my own narrative back to me, with little meaning. While literary devices like metaphors, personifications, etc. were certainly present in the work, they felt very basic and boring, as if the story was told literally.

I then repeated the request, with slight tweaking, using the deep research function.

The result was slightly better, however, still came across quite flat. It was difficult to separate my own expectations to get a genuine reaction out of the final product, as the only way to generate decent results was by holding the chatbot's hand the entire time and guiding it to the correct answer. Subsequently, this whole process ended up taking significantly more time than me writing the original poem.
