Top Platforms!


Spotify Music

One of the top music streaming services in the globe, Spotify was introduced in 2008. It offers a sizable music library with more than 70 million tracks, as well as features for creating custom playlists, podcasts, and live streaming. The "Discover Weekly" playlist, which selects new music based on a user's listening experience, is one of its distinctive features. The platform also gives users the choice between using it for free with advertisements or paying a monthly fee to use the premium edition without ads.



Apple Music

A music streaming programme called Apple Music was introduced by the tech giant Apple in 2015. It has a user interface that is identical to the well-known iTunes app and provides a library of more than 75 million songs. Apple Music offers radio stations, podcasts, and live events in addition to music. "Apple Music 1," a 24-hour global radio station with top DJs and exclusive material, is one intriguing feature.




YouTube Music

The world's largest video-sharing platform, YouTube, debuted its music streaming programme, YouTube Music, in 2018. Both a free and a paid ad-free edition are available. The ability to watch music videos while listening to music is an intriguing feature of YouTube Music, which makes it a fantastic choice for music fans who like visual media. The platform also provides a customised "mixtape" function that creates playlists based on a user's listening preferences.




Amazon Music

The e-commerce behemoth Amazon debuted its music streaming service called Amazon Music in 2007. It provides a variety of subscription choices, including a free version with ads, a Prime membership version, and a premium version with extra features. It boasts a music library of over 75 million songs. Amazon's virtual assistant "Alexa," which enables users to control audio playback using vocal commands, is an intriguing feature of Amazon Music. The platform also has a special function dubbed "X-Ray Lyrics" that shows lyrics of songs as they are playing in real time.




THE DEVELOPMENT


Throughout the last two decades, the online music market has seen a dramatic transition that has changed how we listen to and buy music. Music is now more accessible than ever thanks to the internet and the development of digital technology.

The days of buying actual CDs from record shops are long gone; today, music can be quickly streamed or downloaded from a variety of platforms as mentioned above. Due to this, the online music market has experienced rapid expansion, with sales increasing consistently year after year. With websites like SoundCloud and Bandcamp providing a place for musicians to share their work, the growth of online music has also provided independent artists a better platform to reach more people. The way we engage with and enjoy music has generally been transformed by the internet music industry, and this trend appears to be continuing.

Here is a list of some major people that have had a massive influence on the development:

  • Daniel Ek, founder and CEO of Spotify
  • Jimmy Iovine, co-founder of Beats Electronics and former head of Apple Music
  • Steve Jobs, founder and former CEO of Apple, whose iTunes Store revolutionized digital music sales
  • Lucian Grainge, CEO of Universal Music Group, one of the "big three" record labels
  • Shawn Fanning, co-founder of Napster, the pioneering music file-sharing service
